<!-- INTRODUCTION -->
<section id="intro">
    <h2>Introduction</h2>
    <p>
        Guava leaves are a rich source of bioactive compounds, including polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, 
        and antioxidants. These compounds contribute to multiple health benefits such as regulating blood 
        sugar levels, improving digestion, reducing inflammation, and enhancing immune responses.
    </p>
    <p>
        This project aims to develop <b>functional crackers</b> by incorporating guava leaf powder into 
        traditional cracker formulations. Functional foods are designed not just for basic nutrition, but 
        also to provide added health benefits. By integrating guava leaf powder, we can leverage its 
        medicinal properties in a convenient snack form.
    </p>
    <p>
        Additionally, this innovative approach addresses herbal waste reduction by converting excess guava 
        leaves into a profitable, value-added product. The concept of <b>smart snacks</b> combines 
        nutritional enhancement with modern technologies like digital verification, allowing consumers to 
        trace and verify the authenticity, safety, and health benefits of their food in a digital ecosystem.
    </p>
    <div class="img-center">
        <img src="https://via.placeholder.com/700x400?text=Guava+Leaves+and+Crackers" alt="">
    </div>
</section>

<!-- OBJECTIVES -->
<section id="objectives">
    <h2>Objectives</h2>
    <ul>
        <li>To formulate crackers enriched with varying concentrations of guava leaf powder to evaluate optimal dosage for health benefits.</li>
        <li>To enhance the nutritional profile, antioxidant capacity, and fiber content of traditional crackers.</li>
        <li>To assess sensory characteristics including taste, texture, aroma, crispness, and visual appeal for consumer acceptability.</li>
        <li>To compare the nutritional content and functional properties with conventional crackers.</li>
        <li>To explore shelf-life stability and packaging solutions for smart snacks with digital verification.</li>
        <li>To reduce herbal waste by utilizing guava leaves in a profitable, sustainable way.</li>
        <li>To create a model for functional foods that combines nutrition, health benefits, and technological innovation.</li>
    </ul>
</section>

<!-- METHODOLOGY -->
<section id="methodology">
    <h2>Methodology</h2>
    <p>
        The methodology focuses on integrating scientific procedures and innovative concepts to develop 
        functional crackers:
    </p>
    <div class="card-container">
        <div class="card">Collection & cleaning of fresh guava leaves, ensuring high quality raw material.</div>
        <div class="card">Shade drying to preserve bioactive compounds without degradation.</div>
        <div class="card">Grinding & sieving to create fine guava leaf powder for uniform incorporation.</div>
        <div class="card">Preparation of dough by mixing flour, guava leaf powder, oil, and water in optimal proportions.</div>
        <div class="card">Rolling, cutting, and baking crackers under controlled conditions for texture and crispness.</div>
        <div class="card">Nutritional and sensory evaluation to measure antioxidant content, fiber, taste, and appearance.</div>
        <div class="card">Incorporation of smart packaging and digital verification for traceability and consumer confidence.</div>
        <div class="card">Documentation of results to assess herbal waste reduction and product innovation impact.</div>
    </div>

<!-- PREPARATION -->
<section id="preparation">
    <h2>Preparation Steps</h2>
    <ol>
        <li>Mix all dry ingredients including flour and guava leaf powder.</li>
        <li>Add oil and blend until crumbly.</li>
        <li>Add water gradually and knead into firm dough.</li>
        <li>Roll dough into thin sheets using a roller.</li>
        <li>Cut into square/round cracker shapes.</li>
        <li>Bake at 180°C for 12–15 minutes.</li>
        <li>Cool and store in airtight container.</li>
    </ol>
</section>
